The History of Coca-Cola Cherry

Long before its official introduction in 1985, many diners and drugstore soda fountains dispensed an unofficial version of Cherry Coke by adding cherry-flavored syrup to the Coca-Cola. Coca-Cola tested Cherry Coke on an audience at the 1982 World's Fair. Cherry Coke then entered mainstream production during the summer of 1985.
In 1990, Coca-Cola announced the rollout of a new "fountain-style" Cherry Coke and Diet Cherry Coke with more cherry taste — closer to what people remembered from the soda fountain.
Cherry Coke, which by 2007 had been renamed Coca-Cola Cherry in the U.S. and some other countries, was the third variation of Coca-Cola at that time – the others being Coca-Cola Classic and Diet Coke – and the first flavored Coke.
